How is Ayn Rand's philosophy relevant today?

Ayn Rand believed in the importance of individual choice and happiness. She believed ones happiness came from doing stuff for themselves, not for others. She was strongly against altruism. I think these views have died out sort of, but I think everyone has a little bit of them. At heart, most of the time we are doing stuff because it makes us happy. This is also applicable to politics and special interest groups. Individuals in places of power are only looking out for themselves. They do not care about the people they can hurt through the policies implemented.
